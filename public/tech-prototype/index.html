The System · Cutaway View

Inside the jet array

No rudder. No propeller. No compromise.

The 7-jet linear array replaces every mechanical control surface with directed fluid power. A single flush-mounted intake feeds an axial-flow pump, which pressurizes the distribution manifold at 200 L/s.

Each of the seven independently-commanded nozzles vector their thrust stream to manipulate the boundary layer — the thin sheet of water flowing along the hull surface. By selectively energizing or decelerating this layer, ABLTV generates differential drag that turns the vessel without a single moving surface protruding from the hull.

The Physics

How water becomes steering

The science is simple. The execution is everything.

Boundary Layer Injection

High-velocity water from the jets is injected tangentially into the boundary layer — the region of slowed water adjacent to the hull. This re-energizes the layer, creating a pressure differential that induces yaw.

Differential Thrust Steering

By varying thrust between port and starboard jets (and vectoring individual nozzles up to ±35°), ABLTV creates an asymmetric boundary layer — one side accelerates, the other decelerates — producing a steering moment with zero mechanical surfaces.

Sub-30ms Response

The entire chain — helm input → ECU computation → valve actuation → jet vector change → boundary layer effect — completes in 28 milliseconds. That's faster than a rudder can begin to move.

Fully Graceful Degradation

In a conventional marine drivetrain, losing the single propeller or rudder means total loss of control. ABLTV's 7-of-7 architecture means any single jet — or any three non-adjacent jets — can fail without compromising steering authority. The control loop instantly reallocates thrust vectors across surviving jets, maintaining full maneuverability. The center jet (J4) is oversized by 15% and can compensate for any single port or starboard failure independently.

The ECU, sensors, and power delivery paths are all triple-redundant. No single point of failure can disable the system.
